AOP: Satellite positions and observing

Assume we have xyz position \(\boldsymbol{r}\) of ISS at time zero, and also we know xyz normal vector \(\boldsymbol{n}\), all in an inertial frame. We also know orbital period \(T\). We want to work out where the ISS is in 1 hour, say. Do this by finding a basis consisting of \(\boldsymbol{r}, \boldsymbol{n}, \boldsymbol{a} = \boldsymbol{n} \times \boldsymbol{r}\). In this basis the ISS moves in the \(\boldsymbol{r}\)-\(\boldsymbol{a}\) plane with angular velocity \(2\pi / T\) starting from \((R,0,0)\). Find position in this basis at later time. Convert back to xyz.

To figure out where it is for us in the sky, we need to locate our position, determine SEZ basis vectors, and convert the relative position vector to SEZ, then to Az/El.
