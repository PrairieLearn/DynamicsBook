Tangential/normal basis

Consider a particle moving with position vector $\vec{r}$ and corresponding velocity $\vec{v}$ and acceleration $\vec{a}$. The distance along the path is $s$, which can be found by integrating the speed.

Path length $s$ and speed $v$.

\[\begin{aligned} v &= \dot{s} \\ s &= \int_0^t v \,dt \end{aligned}\]

Basis vectors

Tangential/normal basis vectors.

\[\begin{aligned} \hat{e}_t &= \hat{v} \\ \hat{e}_n &= \frac{\dot{\hat{e}}_t}{\|\dot{\hat{e}}_t\|} \\ \hat{e}_b &= \hat{e}_t \times \hat{e}_n \end{aligned}\]

Curves and curvature

Curvature.

\[\kappa = \frac{1}{\rho}\]

Curvature of parametric curve $\vec{r}(a)$.

\[\kappa = \frac{\|\vec{r}' \times \vec{r}''\|}{\|\vec{r}'\|^3}\]

Curvature of parametric curve $(x(a),y(a))$ in 2D.

\[\kappa = \frac{|x'y'' - y'x''|}{(x'^2 + y'^2)^{3/2}}\]
