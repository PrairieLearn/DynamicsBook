Website: The official class website is http://courses.engr.illinois.edu/tam212/

Description: Kinematics and dynamics of the three-dimensional motion of particles; kinematics and dynamics of the plane motion of rigid bodies; methods of work/energy and impulse/momentum; moving reference frames.

Prerequisites: TAM 210 or TAM 211.

Text: D. J. McGill and W. W. King, Engineering Mechanics: Dynamics, 4th edition, Tichenor Publishing, 2003, ISBN 0742134938.

Alternative texts:

  • R. C. Hibbeler, Engineering Mechanics: Dynamics, 13th edition, Prentice Hall, 2012, ISBN 0132911272, (Amazon).
  • G. Gray, F. Costanzo, and M. Plesha, Engineering Mechanics: Dynamics, 1st edition, McGraw-Hill, 2009, ISBN 0077275543 (Amazon).

Lectures: Prompt and regular attendance at lectures is required. You must attend the lecture section that you are officially enrolled in to obtain iClicker credit.

Discussion sections: Prompt and regular attendance at your discussion section is required. You must attend only the discussion section in which you are enrolled. Most discussion sessions will consist of a group worksheet exercise, which is a high-energy and efficient 50 minute learning experience. Students are randomly assigned each session to a group of three to five people. Each student must submit a completed worksheet, but only one randomly chosen worksheet will be scored from each group, and every student in that group will be given that score. If you are more than 5 minutes late to a discussion session then you will not be permitted to complete the worksheet. Discussion sections start in Week 1 of semester (some of them before the first lecture).

Online homeworks: Weekly online homeworks are due Friday midnight, using the smartPhysics system. There will be 6 questions per week. Two of the questions will have a bonus 10% score if they are submitted by Monday. Another two questions will have a similar bonus if submitted by Wednesday. Late online homework will be accepted with a 50% penalty. The first homework will be due in Week 2 of semester.

Written reports: Each week a written report must be submitted, consisting of a full write-up of one online homework problem (specified on the schedule). This is submitted at the start of the weekly discussion session and will be graded by the TA and returned in the following week's discussion session. Written reports are designed to practice the communication of engineering concepts in writing. They will be graded based on presentation, neatness, correct use of symbols, quality of drawings and diagrams, and clarity of explanation. Correctness of the answer will not be graded. Late written reports will not be accepted. The first reports will be due in Week 2 of semester.

Exams: Exams are held as shown on the class schedule. If you are unable to attend an exam then you must inform your professor by email at the earliest possible opportunity. For non-emergency absences this notification must be at least one week in advance. Conflict-exam arrangements will be announced in the week before each exam.

Participation Star Points: This course is under active development and mistakes will occur in online material and on assessments. For each error, the first student who posts a correct notification to Piazza will receive one Participation Star Point. At the end of semester each Star Point will give a bonus score equivalent to 10% of one homework question (up to a maximum of 5% extra total score). Star Points will also be awarded to students who provide good answers to questions on Piazza and for other meritorious service throughout the semester.

Gradebook: All assessment scores are stored in the My.MechSE gradebook. To have credit awarded, any errors or missing scores must be reported to your professor within 2 weeks of the due date of the assessment item or by the last day of class, whichever is earlier.

Contact and obtaining help: The two main ways to obtain help are online at Piazza, or in person at the Grainger office hours. You can also speak with your professor briefly after lecture. Please do not send email directly to TAs or professors for routine help or absences. In case of emergencies related to exams (e.g., illness) then you should email your professor at the earliest possibly opportunity.

CARE: The Center for Academic Resources in Engineering (CARE) provides extra help and tutoring for TAM 212. Information is available at the CARE website and in person at the CARE offices in Grainger library.

James Scholar honors: Honors credit is available for this course. Please contact your professor for more information.

Absences:

  1. Excuses from assessments will only be given in the following circumstances:
    1. Illness.
    2. Personal crisis (e.g., car accident, required court appearance, death of a close relative).
    3. Required attendance at an official UIUC activity (e.g., varsity athletics, band concert).
  2. In all cases you must complete the TAM Excused Absence Request Form and upload a scan of the official written documentation explaining your absence.
  3. In cases (a) or (b) an official excuse letter from the Dean on Duty must be submitted via the online form within 2 weeks of the due date of the missed assessment, but no later than reading day. In cases of extended or unusual illness, late submission of excuse documentation will be considered.
  4. In case (c) an official letter from the designated university official must be submitted via the online form at least one week prior to the due date of the missed assessment.
  5. The dropped assessments (see Grading above) are intended for excused absences. If there are more excused absences for a given assessment type than the standard number of dropped assessments for that type, then the number of dropped assessments of that type will be increased to be equal to the number of excused absences for that type. For example, if the two lowest homework scores are normally dropped, but you have four excused absences for homeworks, then the four lowest homework scores will be dropped.
  6. If you will not be able to sit an exam due to illness or any other reason, you must send email to your professor at the earliest possible opportunity. Excused exams will be replaced by a weighted average of the other exam scores at the end of semester.
  7. Notwithstanding the above, at your professor's discretion you may be required to make up any excused work or attend substitute instruction or assessment.

Academic integrity, harassment, and discrimination: Academic integrity infractions, harassment, and discrimination of any kind will not be tolerated. See Article 1, Part 4 of the Student Code and the Campus Conduct Policy. Please report any concerns immediately to your professor.
