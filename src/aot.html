AOT: Sidereal and solar time

Is the inertial angular period of the Earth 24 hours? No, it’s actually \(23\rm\ h\), \(56\rm\ min\). \(24\rm\ h\) is the time from middday to midday, but during that time the Earth moves \(1/365\) around its orbit.

Solar days are measured with respect to the Sun, sidereal days with respect to the stars (inertial).

Did you know?

The universal time standard for clocks and time is Coordinated Universal Time (UTC), which replaced the earlier Greenwich Mean Time (GMT) standard. The fact that UTC is wrong as an abbreviation is due to a disagreement between English and French speakers. English speakers wanted CUT (Coordinated Universal Time), while French speakers wanted TUC (Temps Universel Coordonné). Neither side would back down, and in the end it was agreed to use UTC so that everyone could be equally unhappy.
