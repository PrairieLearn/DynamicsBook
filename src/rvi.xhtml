Vector identities

Cross product anti-symmetry

\[\begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{u} \times \boldsymbol{v} = - \boldsymbol{v} \times \boldsymbol{u}\end{aligned}\]

Write out both sides in components.

Cross product self-annihilation

\[\begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{u} \times \boldsymbol{u} = 0\end{aligned}\]

From anti-symmetry.

Cross product bi-linearity

\[\begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{a} \times (\boldsymbol{b} + \boldsymbol{c}) &= \boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b} + \boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{c} \\ (\boldsymbol{a} + \boldsymbol{b}) \times \boldsymbol{c} &= \boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{c} + \boldsymbol{b} \times \boldsymbol{c} \\ \boldsymbol{a} \times (\beta \boldsymbol{b}) &= \beta (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b}) = (\beta \boldsymbol{a}) \times \boldsymbol{b}\end{aligned}\]

Write out both sides in components.

The scalar triple product is \(\boldsymbol{u} \cdot (\boldsymbol{v} \times \boldsymbol{w})\), which gives the volume of the parallelepiped defined by \(\boldsymbol{u}, \boldsymbol{v}, \boldsymbol{w}\). It satisfies:

Scalar triple product formula

\[\begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{u} \cdot (\boldsymbol{v} \times \boldsymbol{w}) = \boldsymbol{v} \cdot (\boldsymbol{w} \times \boldsymbol{u}) = \boldsymbol{w} \cdot (\boldsymbol{u} \times \boldsymbol{v})\end{aligned}\]

Write first two expressions out in components and check that they give the same result:

\[\begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{u} \cdot (\boldsymbol{v} \times \boldsymbol{w}) &= (u_1 \,\hat{\imath} + u_2 \,\hat{\jmath} + u_3 \,\hat{k}) \cdot \big( (v_1 \,\hat{\imath} + v_2 \,\hat{\jmath} + v_3 \,\hat{k}) \times (w_1 \,\hat{\imath} + w_2 \,\hat{\jmath} + w_3 \,\hat{k}) \big) \\ &= (u_1 \,\hat{\imath} + u_2 \,\hat{\jmath} + u_3 \,\hat{k}) \cdot \big( (v_2 w_3 - v_3 w_2) \,\hat{\imath} + (v_3 w_1 - v_1 w_3) \,\hat{\jmath} + (v_1 w_2 - v_2 w_1) \,\hat{k} \big) \\ &= u_1 v_2 w_3 - u_1 v_3 w_2 + u_2 v_3 w_1 - u_2 v_1 w_3 + u_3 v_1 w_2 - u_3 v_2 w_1 \\ \boldsymbol{v} \cdot (\boldsymbol{w} \times \boldsymbol{u}) &= (v_1 \,\hat{\imath} + v_2 \,\hat{\jmath} + v_3 \,\hat{k}) \cdot \big( (w_1 \,\hat{\imath} + w_2 \,\hat{\jmath} + w_3 \,\hat{k}) \times (u_1 \,\hat{\imath} + u_2 \,\hat{\jmath} + u_3 \,\hat{k}) \big) \\ &= (v_1 \,\hat{\imath} + v_2 \,\hat{\jmath} + v_3 \,\hat{k}) \cdot \big( (w_2 u_3 - w_3 u_2) \,\hat{\imath} + (w_3 u_1 - w_1 u_3) \,\hat{\jmath} + (w_1 u_2 - w_2 u_1) \,\hat{k} \big) \\ &= v_1 w_2 u_3 - v_1 w_3 u_2 + v_2 w_3 u_1 - v_2 w_1 u_3 + v_3 w_1 u_2 - v_3 w_2 u_1\end{aligned}\]

The third expression is also the same.

The vector triple product is \(\boldsymbol{a} \times (\boldsymbol{b} \times \boldsymbol{c})\). It satisfies:

Vector triple product expansion

\[\begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{a} \times (\boldsymbol{b} \times \boldsymbol{c}) = (\boldsymbol{a} \cdot \boldsymbol{c}) \boldsymbol{b} - (\boldsymbol{a} \cdot \boldsymbol{b}) \boldsymbol{c}\end{aligned}\]

In components:

\[\begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{a} \times (\boldsymbol{b} \times \boldsymbol{c}) &= (a_1 \,\hat{\imath} + a_2 \,\hat{\jmath} + a_3 \,\hat{k}) \times \Big( (b_1 \,\hat{\imath} + b_2 \,\hat{\jmath} + b_3 \,\hat{k}) \times (c_1 \,\hat{\imath} + c_2 \,\hat{\jmath} + c_3 \,\hat{k}) \Big) \\ &= (a_1 \,\hat{\imath} + a_2 \,\hat{\jmath} + a_3 \,\hat{k}) \times \Big( (b_2 c_3 - b_3 c_2) \,\hat{\imath} + (b_3 c_1 - b_1 c_3) \,\hat{\jmath} + (b_1 c_2 - b_2 c_1) \,\hat{k} \Big) \\ &= \Big(a_2 (b_1 c_2 - b_2 c_1) - a_3 (b_3 c_1 - b_1 c_3)\Big) \,\hat{\imath} \\ &\quad+ \Big(a_3 (b_2 c_3 - b_3 c_2) - a_1 (b_1 c_2 - b_2 c_1)\Big) \,\hat{\jmath} \\ &\quad+ \Big(a_1 (b_3 c_1 - b_1 c_3) - a_2 (b_2 c_3 - b_3 c_2)\Big) \,\hat{k} \\ &= (a_2 b_1 c_2 - a_2 b_2 c_1 - a_3 b_3 c_1 + a_3 b_1 c_3) \,\hat{\imath} \\ &\quad+ (a_3 b_2 c_3 - a_3 b_3 c_2 - a_1 b_1 c_2 + a_1 b_2 c_1) \,\hat{\jmath} \\ &\quad+ (a_1 b_3 c_1 - a_1 b_1 c_3 - a_2 b_2 c_3 + a_2 b_3 c_2) \,\hat{k} \\ &= (a_1 b_1 c_1 + a_2 b_1 c_2 + a_3 b_1 c_3 - a_1 b_1 c_1 - a_2 b_2 c_1 - a_3 b_3 c_1) \,\hat{\imath} \\ &\quad+ (a_1 b_2 c_1 + a_2 b_2 c_2 + a_3 b_2 c_3 - a_1 b_1 c_2 - a_2 b_2 c_2 - a_3 b_3 c_2) \,\hat{\jmath} \\ &\quad+ (a_1 b_3 c_1 + a_2 b_3 c_2 + a_3 b_3 c_3 - a_1 b_1 c_3 - a_2 b_2 c_3 - a_3 b_3 c_3) \,\hat{k} \\ &= (a_1 b_1 + a_2 c_2 + a_3 c_3) b_1 \,\hat{\imath} + (a_1 c_1 + a_2 c_2 + a_3 c_3) b_2 \,\hat{\jmath} + (a_1 c_1 + a_2 c_2 + a_3 c_3) b_3 \,\hat{k} \\ &\quad- (a_1 b_1 + a_2 b_2 + a_3 b_3) c_1 \,\hat{\imath} - (a_1 b_1 + a_2 b_2 + a_3 b_3) c_2 \,\hat{\jmath} - (a_1 b_1 + a_2 b_2 + a_3 b_3) c_3 \,\hat{k} \\ &= (\boldsymbol{a} \cdot \boldsymbol{c}) \boldsymbol{b} - (\boldsymbol{a} \cdot \boldsymbol{b}) \boldsymbol{c}\end{aligned}\]

Cross product orthogonality

\[\begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b} \text{ is orthogonal to } \boldsymbol{a} \text{ and } \boldsymbol{b}\end{aligned}\]

Follows immediately from the scalar triple product formula:

\[\begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{a} \cdot (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b}) = \boldsymbol{b} \cdot (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{a}) = 0\end{aligned}\]

and similarly for \(\boldsymbol{b}\).

Binet-Cauchy identity

\[\begin{aligned} (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b}) \cdot (\boldsymbol{c} \times \boldsymbol{d}) = (\boldsymbol{a} \cdot \boldsymbol{c})(\boldsymbol{b} \cdot \boldsymbol{d}) - (\boldsymbol{a} \cdot \boldsymbol{d})(\boldsymbol{b} \cdot \boldsymbol{c})\end{aligned}\]

From the scalar triple product formula and the vector triple product expansion:

\[\begin{aligned} (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b}) \cdot (\boldsymbol{c} \times \boldsymbol{d}) &= \boldsymbol{c} \cdot \big( \boldsymbol{d} \times (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b}) \big) \\ &= \boldsymbol{c} \cdot \big( (\boldsymbol{d} \cdot \boldsymbol{b}) \boldsymbol{a} - (\boldsymbol{d} \cdot \boldsymbol{a}) \boldsymbol{b} \big) \\ &= (\boldsymbol{d} \cdot \boldsymbol{b}) (\boldsymbol{c} \cdot \boldsymbol{a}) - (\boldsymbol{d} \cdot \boldsymbol{a}) (\boldsymbol{c} \cdot \boldsymbol{b})\end{aligned}\]

Lagrange's identity

\[\begin{aligned} \| \boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b} \|^2 = \|\boldsymbol{a}\|^2 \|\boldsymbol{b}\|^2 - (\boldsymbol{a} \cdot \boldsymbol{b})^2\end{aligned}\]

Follows immediately from the Binet-Cauchy identity.

Cross product length

\[\begin{aligned} \| \boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b} \| = \|\boldsymbol{a}\| \|\boldsymbol{b}\| \sin\theta\end{aligned}\]

From Lagrangeâ€™s identity:

\[\begin{aligned} \| \boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b} \|^2 &= \|\boldsymbol{a}\|^2 \|\boldsymbol{b}\|^2 - (\boldsymbol{a} \cdot \boldsymbol{b})^2 \\ &= \|\boldsymbol{a}\|^2 \|\boldsymbol{b}\|^2 - (\|\boldsymbol{a}\| \|\boldsymbol{b}\| \cos\theta)^2 \\ &= \|\boldsymbol{a}\|^2 \|\boldsymbol{b}\|^2 (1 - \cos^2\theta) \\ &= \|\boldsymbol{a}\|^2 \|\boldsymbol{b}\|^2 \sin^2\theta\end{aligned}\]

Jacobi's identity

\[\begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{a} \times (\boldsymbol{b} \times \boldsymbol{c}) + \boldsymbol{b} \times (\boldsymbol{c} \times \boldsymbol{a}) + \boldsymbol{c} \times (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b}) = 0\end{aligned}\]

Using the vector triple product expansion:

\[\begin{aligned} &\boldsymbol{a} \times (\boldsymbol{b} \times \boldsymbol{c}) + \boldsymbol{b} \times (\boldsymbol{c} \times \boldsymbol{a}) + \boldsymbol{c} \times (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b}) \\ &\qquad= (\boldsymbol{a} \cdot \boldsymbol{c}) \boldsymbol{b} - (\boldsymbol{a} \cdot \boldsymbol{b}) \boldsymbol{c} + (\boldsymbol{b} \cdot \boldsymbol{a}) \boldsymbol{c} - (\boldsymbol{b} \cdot \boldsymbol{c}) \boldsymbol{a} + (\boldsymbol{c} \cdot \boldsymbol{b}) \boldsymbol{a} - (\boldsymbol{c} \cdot \boldsymbol{a}) \boldsymbol{b} \\ &\qquad= 0\end{aligned}\]

Vector quadruple product expansion

\[\begin{aligned} (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b}) \times (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{c}) = \big( \boldsymbol{a} \cdot (\boldsymbol{b} \times \boldsymbol{c}) \big) \boldsymbol{a}\end{aligned}\]

Take \(\boldsymbol{d} = (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b}) \times (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{c})\). Then \(\boldsymbol{d}\) is in the \(\boldsymbol{a},\boldsymbol{b}\) plane and in the \(\boldsymbol{a},\boldsymbol{c}\) plane, so it is a scalar multiple of \(\boldsymbol{a}\). We use the scalar triple product formula and the vector triple product expansion to compute:

\[\begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{d} \cdot \boldsymbol{a} &= \boldsymbol{a} \cdot \big( (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b}) \times (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{c}) \big) \\ &= (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{c}) \cdot \big( \boldsymbol{a} \times (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{b}) \big) \\ &= (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{c}) \cdot \big( (\boldsymbol{a} \cdot \boldsymbol{b}) \boldsymbol{a} - (\boldsymbol{a} \cdot \boldsymbol{a}) \boldsymbol{b} \big) \\ &= - (\boldsymbol{a} \cdot \boldsymbol{a}) \boldsymbol{b} \cdot (\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{c}) \\ &= (\boldsymbol{a} \cdot \boldsymbol{a}) \big( \boldsymbol{a} \cdot (\boldsymbol{b} \times \boldsymbol{c}) \big)\end{aligned}\]

Then

\[\begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{d} = \operatorname{Proj}(\boldsymbol{d}, \boldsymbol{a}) = \left(\frac{\boldsymbol{d} \cdot \boldsymbol{a}}{\boldsymbol{a} \cdot \boldsymbol{a}}\right) \boldsymbol{a} = \big( \boldsymbol{a} \cdot (\boldsymbol{b} \times \boldsymbol{c}) \big) \boldsymbol{a}\end{aligned}\]
