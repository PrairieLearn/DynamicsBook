Cylindrical coordinates

See GCP p. 119 for a picture.

Coordinates:

\[\begin{aligned} (r, \theta, z)\end{aligned}\]

To Cartesian:

\[\begin{aligned} x &= r \cos\theta \\ y &= r \sin\theta \\ z &= z\end{aligned}\]

From Cartesian:

\[\begin{aligned} r &= \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} \\ \theta &= \operatorname{atan2}(y, x) \\ z &= z\end{aligned}\]

Basis vectors:

\[\begin{aligned} \,\hat{e}_r &= \cos\theta \,\hat{\imath} + \sin\theta \,\hat{\jmath} \\ \,\hat{e}_{\theta} &= - \sin\theta \,\hat{\imath} + \cos\theta \,\hat{\jmath} \\ \,\hat{k} &= \,\hat{k}\end{aligned}\]

Angular velocity:

\[\begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{\omega} &= \dot\theta \,\hat{k}\end{aligned}\]

Basis vector derivatives:

\[\begin{aligned} \dot{\hat{e}}_r &= \boldsymbol{\omega} \times \hat{e}_r = \dot\theta \,\hat{e}_{\theta} \\ \dot{\hat{e}}_{\theta} &= \boldsymbol{\omega} \times \hat{e}_{\theta} = - \dot\theta \,\hat{e}_r \\ \dot{\hat{k}} &= \boldsymbol{\omega} \times \hat{k} = 0\end{aligned}\]

Position, velocity, and acceleration:

\[\begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{r} &= r \,\hat{e}_r + z \,\hat{k} \\ \boldsymbol{v} &= \dot r \,\hat{e}_r + r \dot\theta \,\hat{e}_{\theta} + \dot z \,\hat{k} \\ \boldsymbol{a} &= (\ddot r - r \dot{\theta}^2) \,\hat{e}_r + (r \ddot\theta + 2 \dot{r} \dot\theta) \,\hat{e}_{\theta} + \ddot z \,\hat{k}\end{aligned}\]
