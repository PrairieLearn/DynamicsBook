Question: You walking from one end of an airport to the other, with several moving walkways that you will use. You want to stop for a brief time to get your phone out of your bag. To minimize your total walking time, should you stop when on a moving walkway or when stationary, or doesn't it matter?

Question: You are riding your bike from home to the store and back, and there is a constant wind blowing, so that on the way there you have a headwind, but on the way back you have a corresponding tailwind. Assume that the wind adds a constant offset to your speed. Are you slower, faster, or the same compared to if there was no wind at all?

Question: What if the wind doubles your speed when it is a tailwind, but halves it when it's a headwind. Are you faster or slower or the same?

Question: Two runners are competing in a (slightly short) 40 km long marathon. Fred runs the first 20 km at 20 km/h and the second 20 km at 10 km/h. Bill runs for exactly half his total time at 20 km/h and for the second half of his total time at 10 km/h. Who wins? Who has an average speed of 15 km/h?
