AOX: How do sextants work?

A sextant measures angles between things. Some common useful angles to measure are the elevation of the sun, elevation of stars, horizontal angles between known points on the land, etc. By measuring the elevation of the sun at solar noon, or Polaris at any time in the night, the current latitude can be immediately determined. By measuring the angle between two points on the land separated by a known distance, the distance offshore can be computed.

By measuring the “lunar distance” between the moon and a fixed star, the universal time can be determined, thus giving longitude.
